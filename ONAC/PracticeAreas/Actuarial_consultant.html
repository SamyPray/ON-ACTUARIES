Actuarial Consultant

Why do we need Actuarial Consultant?

An actuarial consultant has the professional skill to assess risks, to help the insurer shape its policies for things Ike premium prices and other important information.

We perform actuarial valuations for funding policy purposes or in compliance with accounting and disclosure requirements. The actuarial valuation for funding purposes provides the recommended retirement plan contributions to ensure that retirement benefit obligations are met when due, without undue financial stress on the client/employer.

An actuarial consultant has a good understanding of the insurance business, uses the skills of statistics, economics and probability, is excellent in both qualitative and quantitative data analysis and can deal with data during analysis. Our actuarial consultant services are and are not limited to:

  • To develop research design and tools, sample size and statistical analysis plans and statistical modeling.
  • To predict and develop real time analysis of data to get desired insight for the company
  • To have the ability to calculate claim reserves for monthly, quarterly and yearly management reporting.
  • To have the ability to calculate claim reserves for monthly, quarterly and yearly management reporting.
  • To have ability to calculate premium forecasts and projections plus the ability to model cash flows, risk exposure and portfolio performance over time.
  • To have ability to prepare a report regarding various aspects of insurance coverage such as premium analysis, reserves for internal use by management
  • To have ability to use advanced statistics and modeling to understand insurance data and analyze results from models.
  • To have ability to calculate how insurance policies for different types are likely to pay out and establish which line of business are profitable to the company.
  • To have ability to work on Actuarial reporting, experience monitoring, Actuarial risk control, capital management as well as insurance product management
  • To have ability to collaborate with fellow staff members to discuss any data problems that may arise and develop potential solutions.
